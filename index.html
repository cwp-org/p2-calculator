Assumptions & Data Sources
  • Fertilizer type: 10-6-4 (N-P-K by weight) applied for perennial plantings. One application per year in spring.
  • Application rate: 1 lb of fertilizer per 100 sq ft of planting area (equivalent to 0.1 lbs N per 100 sf). Source: UMD Extension.
  • Soil Type A/B: 40% of nutrients applied become runoff. Soil Type C/D: 70% become runoff.
  • Establishment period: Fertilizer assumed to be applied only during the first 3 years of plant establishment.
  • Nursery — applications: 2 per year for native plants (during active growth stages).
  • Nursery — fertilizer loss: 15–30% typical loss; default 30%. Varies by irrigation method and fertilizer type.
  • Nursery — runoff reach: 50–90% of leached nutrients reach waterways; default 50%. Varies by drainage system and surface permeability.
  • Surface area per plant is calculated as a circle: π × (spacing ÷ 2)². Spacing represents root zone or typical container size.

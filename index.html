Assumptions & Data Sources
  • Equipment specifications (battery size, gas HP, gallons/hr): Massachusetts Commercial Battery-Powered Electric Lawn and Garden Equipment Calculator — Leading by Example, Operational Services Division, and Quiet Communities.
  • Battery cost formula: Battery kWh × Batteries × Hours/yr × (1 + Charging Loss) × Electricity Rate (¢/kWh) ÷ 100. Charging efficiency loss default: 10%.
  • Battery CO₂ formula: Battery kWh × Batteries × Hours/yr × (1 + Charging Loss) × State CO₂ Rate (lb/MWh) × 0.001. Uses state non-baseload grid emission rate.
  • Gas cost formula: Gallons/hr × Hours/yr × Gas Price ($/gal).
  • Gas CO₂ formula: Gas HP × Hours/yr × HP CO₂ Constant (g/hp·hr) ÷ 453.592 g/lb. HP CO₂ constants derived from equipment emission factors.
  • State electricity prices (¢/kWh, Feb 2026 YTD by sector — Residential, Commercial, Industrial, All Sectors): U.S. EIA Table 5.6.b.
  • State grid CO₂ equivalent emission rates (lb/MWh, non-baseload, 2023): EPA eGRID 2023 Detailed Data.
  • Gasoline Spillage Reduction (lb/yr): = Gallons of Gas Per Hour × Hours Used Per Year × 0.0599 lb/gal × 0.06 runoff delivery fraction. 0.0599 lb/gal = sum of refueling spillage (0.0375), pump dispensing (0.0007), and transport spillage (0.0217) for residential portable fuel containers; sources: EPA NONROAD/PFC, EPA AP-42 §5.2, EPA PFC. The 6% runoff delivery fraction is the screening-level petroleum runoff proxy from the Puget Sound toxics loading assessment.
  • Puerto Rico & U.S. Virgin Islands: Electricity rates not available in EIA Table 5.6.b; calculator uses U.S. average rate across all states (R: 18.15, C: 14.74, I: 11.39, A: 15.20 ¢/kWh) for both territories. Grid CO₂ rate: Puerto Rico uses eGRID 2023 value (1,642.029 lb/MWh); USVI uses U.S. average (1,366.670 lb/MWh) since eGRID does not report USVI separately.
  • Positive cost/CO₂ change = savings from switching to battery. Negative = increase. Results represent annual impacts for the hours entered.

Assumptions & Data Sources
  • Peak Watering Month: Month with highest monthly ETo value from zip code climate data (NOAA/PRISM normals, extracted from Rainfall.xlsx).
  • Part 2 — Supplemental Irrigation: LWR (gal/peak mo) = Area (sf) × max(0, [ETo × PF] − P50) × 0.623. If mulched: × 0.83 (17% soil moisture retention). Turf baseline PF = 0.70. Conversion factor 0.623 converts in·sf to gallons.
  • Part 3 — Supplemental Fertilizer: Annual N (lbs N/yr) = (Area sf ÷ 1,000) × NF. N Runoff (lbs/yr) = Annual N × R, where R = fraction of applied N converted to surface runoff by NRCS Hydrologic Soil Group (Watershed Treatment Model, CWP 2010). Turf NF = 4.0 lbs N/1,000 sf/yr. If no synthetic fertilizer selected: designed N = 0 (100% reduction).
  • Part 4 — Plastic Reduction: # Plants = Area ÷ [0.866 × (spacing in ÷ 12)²] (triangular grid). Container plants: 0.38 lbs plastic/plant. Seedlings/plugs: 0.48 lbs/72-cell tray (A.M. Leonard Horticultural Tool Supply). Plastic avoided = % bare root × total plastic.
  • Plant Factors (PF) and Nitrogen Application Factors (NF) are derived from an informal literature review by CWP. Full background, citations, and methodology are available in the Landscape Calculator Background Research (download .docx) document.
  • Mulch moisture retention (17%): ISA Arboriculture literature review (2025) — Does Excess Mulch Depth lead to poor tree growth and condition?
  • NRCS Hydrologic Soil Group N runoff fractions: A = 0.75%, B = 2.5%, C = 17.5%, D = 22.5% (CWP Watershed Treatment Model, 2010).
  • Additional sources: Rutgers NJAES FS633; Purdue University Turfgrass Science; WUCOLS Plant Factor Database (UC ANR); EPA WaterSense Water Budget Tool; NRCS Hydrologic Soil Groups.

Assumptions & Data Sources
  • GHG — Spreader Vehicle (Application Phase): CO₂ g/mile = 8,887 ÷ MPG (EPA formula for gasoline). Application MTCO₂e/ton = (CO₂ g/mile × 2,000) ÷ (application rate lbs/LM × 1,000,000). Source: EPA Greenhouse Gas Rating.
  • GHG — NaCl Production Phase: 0.270 MTCO₂e/ton (global LCA analysis). Source: ProBas/Umweltbundesamt LCA database.
  • GHG — NaCl Transportation Phase: 0.00136 MTCO₂e/ton. Source: Table 8, EPA Emission Factors for GHG Inventories (2024).
  • Chloride Loading: 884 g Cl/m²/yr observed loading rate for road surfaces. Area: lane miles × 5,880 m²/lane-mile (1 mile × 12 ft lane). Annual metric tons Cl = 884 × area ÷ 1,000,000. Alt de-icer contributes 0 Cl. Source: Bermarija et al., 2023 (EJRH).
  • Social Cost of Carbon: $190/MTCO₂e applied to total lifecycle emissions per ton × seasonal tons applied. Source: EPA Report on the Social Cost of Greenhouse Gases (2023).
  • Infrastructure Damage (Road Salt): $3,341/ton. Source: Fortin (2014), Real Cost of Road Salt Use, Twin Cities Metropolitan Area.
  • Salt Application Rate Default: 150 lbs/lane mile/event. Source: FHWA Manual of Practice for an Effective Anti-icing Program (1996).
  • Alternative De-icer: CO₂/ton (lifecycle) and infrastructure damage $/ton are user-provided inputs. SCC for alt de-icer is calculated as $190 × user CO₂/ton × seasonal tons. Chloride loading = 0. GHG differences between road salt and alt de-icers are often modest due to similar production-phase emissions.

Assumptions & Data Sources
  • Water Bottles (PET): 9.32 g/bottle (IBWA, NAPCOR) · 0.12 kg CO₂-eq/bottle (NIH lifecycle report) · 1.4 gal water/bottle (IBWA) · $0.02/bottle (Science Direct). GHG does not include transportation impacts.
  • Grocery Bags (HDPE): 6 g/bag · 0.027 kg CO₂e/bag · 0.04 gal/bag (150 mL) · $0.006/bag (Duke 2025; Social Cost of Carbon $190/MT CO₂).
  • Straws (PP): 0.5 g/straw · 0.00146 kg CO₂/straw · 0.03 gal/straw. Cost via SCC $190/MT CO₂.
  • Cutlery (PP): 2.49 g/piece · 0.00252 kg CO₂/piece · 0.19 gal/piece (lifecycle range 0.16–0.22 gal). Cost via SCC $190/MT CO₂.
  • Social Cost of Carbon: $190 per metric ton CO₂ used for cost savings where direct cost data is unavailable.
  • Water consumption estimates are conservative figures; actual savings may vary by product source and supply chain.

Assumptions & Data Sources
  • Plastic per 1-gal container: 2.67 oz — average of a variety of typical plant container weights at the 1-gallon pot size.
  • HDPE emission factor: 1.92 kg CO₂/kg — lifecycle GHG for virgin high-density polyethylene. (Source)
  • Recycled HDPE emission factor: 0.24 kg CO₂/kg — lifecycle GHG for recycled HDPE, significantly lower than virgin material. (Source)
  • Unit conversions: 35.274 oz = 1 kg; 1 kg = 2.20462 lbs.
  • Results reflect emissions avoided by eliminating plastic container production. Transportation emissions are not included.
